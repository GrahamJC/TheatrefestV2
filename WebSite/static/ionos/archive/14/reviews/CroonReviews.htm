Noir: a Dick Privet Mystery

Croon productions


The atmosphere of the mystery surrounding Dick Privet is set as soon as you walk into the venue: music is a key component in setting the scene for this performance. ‘DETH’, as Dick would spell it, is the order of the day as he is called upon to uncover the whereabouts of Jonny Volent. Noir: A Dick Privet Mystery features live puppetry. The actors on stage could be distracting, it was difficult to not look at them as they guided the characters around the space. The facial expressions of the actors signpost the characters’ feelings and this was often the source of the show’s light-hearted moments.

Appropriately, the act is prop-heavy which can slow down the plot sometimes, but puppeteering was not a skill that I expected to see and the performers treated the leads attentively as they travelled their guided path. The set is made from former flight cases and suitcases, making the journey to pre-war America all the more engaging.

A highly commendable show that is sure to charm and impress.
Jackson Briggs, The Daily Fringe
