Chet Baker, Love

Brite Theater


Chet Baker, Love is a one woman show, revealing an apparently true relationship from the performer's past, and its continued impact upon her life. The performance raises themes obsession, honesty, loss and that unobtainable ideal of romantic love that has consumed so many teenage lives. Reflecting upon this from the position of adulthood, Kolbrún Sigfúsdóttir produces a carefully weighted, skilled performance that uses direct address, occasional song, some wonderful projections and a great many lists to draw the audience into the relationship being reconstructed on stage.

The piece questions the sincerity of both the relationship being examined and the sincerity of sharing it through performance, drawing an intelligent parallel between the two. I wonder if this idea could have been developed further; the skill with which the piece is constructed and the awareness of the performance style imply that the work is well within her limits.

Chet Baker, Love offers a multi-layered and subtly nuanced and skilful performance.
Joe Burford-Redgrove, The Daily Fringe
