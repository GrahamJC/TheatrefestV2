Hansel & Gretel

Wolf + Water Arts Co and Many Directions


Hansel and Gretel is a perfectly woven, moving and thought provoking piece of theatre. The scene is set through ensemble moments which create images with the help of simple, but effective masks, costume and set. The pacing of the performance is perfect – a gradual visual opening gives time for the audience to process and read into what is being created in front of them. Small, subtle moments of this opening section are not glossed over, instead images are left to settle and breathe. Meanwhile a projection and musical score drives the performance forwards towards the story and the dialogue, which is given more importance by the beauty of the introduction. There are many highlights to this performance; the lost passage is slightly confusing and odd and this style perfectly reflects the story as the protagonists become more and more lost. The ensemble work is strong and the individual performances of Hansel and Gretel, their Mother and Father and the Witch are compelling.

The music and projection is used to great effect, it enhances and avoids being a distraction from the excellent performance on stage. The writing perfectly weaves traditional rhythmic repetition found in fairy tales, modern language and poetic verse spoken out across the audience. The performances are clear, confident, straight-forward and honest. This poignant performance deepens the significance of the the tale of Hansel and Gretel. A truly profound and moving piece of work.
Jess Burford-Redgrove, The Daily Fringe
