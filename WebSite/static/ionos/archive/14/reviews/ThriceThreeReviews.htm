Broken Angels is a piece which looks at the reality of life with an eating disorder. It is a young girl we see on stage, the stereotyped image of the sufferer, if you will. Personally I felt it important however that her voice and ultimately the story relayed attends to the atypical. It is made clear that the disease takes on many forms and impact young and old, male and female, from all walks of life. Knowing the material was based on fact added intensity, which was furthered by the mesmerising performances given by the three actors. A special mention should be given to the understudy who stepped in at the last minute to deliver a truly beautifully authentic interpretation which really added to the performance for me, when it could so easily have detracted. Clearly this is a very carefully researched and considered piece. Devastating in content and delivery, it has been deliberately conceived with the very clear intention of genuine education and awareness promotion.

The topic of mental health and eating disorders is particular is highly contentious. I would not have chosen to see this piece if I hadn't been reviewing it. Irrespective of one's personal experience and indeed opinion, I would assert that this is a piece that deserves to be seen. Unavoidably this is a piece that is not uniformly appropriate and will challenge and impact uniquely according to the individual. However I personally would urge you to take my lead, choose something that looks like it might not be for you and go see it. I hope you, like me here, will find your experience of this year's festival immeasurably better for it.
Jemma Clancy, The Daily Fringe
