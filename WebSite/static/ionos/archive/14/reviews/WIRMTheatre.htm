First Steps

WIRM Theatre


Joe Vaughan and Tom Vinall each present a personal investigation into journeys. More particularly they both offer different yet supporting performances and insights into journeys made on foot.

In a lecture-style performance, the audience is directly given plenty of material to consider on the topic of journeying through life and the impact of that journey on the world. At moments the lecture style and sheer volume of information means that this performance is more interesting than entertaining, but the skill of Joe's writing, Tom's dynamic presentation and the playful interaction between the two goes some way to redress this balance. Tom enlivens his account with interludes of movement and film. He uses song and poetry in a funny and touching way. Joe offers a very focussed performance which holds the audience's attention through beautiful writing and moments of humour.

The set is simple and effective. It places the performers clearly in the context of the piece. They clearly feel comfortable with the set and props that surround them, which reflects the honest and personal nature of the content.

At times emotional and at others nostalgic, this performance takes the audience along with the performers on their respective journeys. The audience are left with more insight into what it is to set out for a destination and what it is to finally arrive.
Jess Burford-Redgrove, The Daily Fringe
