Threnody for the Sky Children

Jack Dean


Jack pulls off a masterful juxtaposition between the meta narrative of a post apocalyptic American led dystopia, reflections on young love frustrated by legalistic religion, and numerous references of a youth misspent on scifi, alternative thinking, and dreaming of a brighter future that never arrived.

A one man cast, Jack sustains attention with amazement, despair, comedy, and honest observation. An extraordinary outpouring that will leave you smiling, laughing, crying, and thinking.

Just Brilliant.
Paul Reisbach

 

Threnody for the Sky Children is a beautifully written on man show. Jack Dean took us into James' parents attic, to tell a touching and funny story. The performance cleverly parodies emotional self obsession and the dramatisation of personal emotional events through flashes of over the top drama and an apocalyptic theme running throughout. On top of this there is also funny and percpetive political and social commentary. In fact there is a huge amount of content in this performance, which sometimes results in some of the most beautiful moments being over slightly too quickly, ideas and images are not always given time needed for the audience to read into. This is only a shame because everything is carefully crafted and precious in this production - the use of uplighting, shadow, puppetry, projection and music are all stunning. The set is simple but effective, boxes of toys and lamps create both a believable attic and a toolbox for Jack Dean to give further life to the imagined worlds that his vivid writing creates.

Threnody for the Sky Children is an excellent show. A Fringe Theatrefest must see.
Jess Burford-Redgrove, The Daily Fringe
