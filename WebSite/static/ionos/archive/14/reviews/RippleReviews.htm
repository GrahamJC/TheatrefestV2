Jophnny Mathis Killed Our Christmas

Ripple


Johnny Mathis Killed Our Christmas is a a piece rich with bleak drama and poignant humour. Following a couple's Christmas ritual through the years, revisiting memories, both joyful and painful, it presents a sensitive look at dementia and memory loss.

Ruth Mitchell and Dennis Frood deliver strong performances, opening a window onto the lives of the characters they portray. The play is presented in a cyclical manner, carefully revealing the characters' past and their changing perceptions of personal history. While some memories fade, others gain greater significance. Information is given at a considered and deliberate pace, allowing the audience the space and time to reflect upon the themes and issues raised.

The overall aesthetic is simple and effective, providing a clear point of focus for the audience. The somewhat static nature of the piece feels appropriate for a play focusing on snapshots of the characters' history. However, I wonder if dispersing the action and simple set around the stage as the play progresses might better reflect some of the content.

Overall, Johnny Mathis Killed Our Christmas contains two touching and well crafted performances, reflecting sensitively on memory and the loss of it. Well worth seeing.
Joe Burford-Redgrove, The Daily Fringe
