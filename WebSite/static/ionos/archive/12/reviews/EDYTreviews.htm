Snarks

Entertainingly Different Youth Theatre


Lewis Carroll’s nonsense poem is innovatively staged and confidently acted by a cast performing well beyond their years, in a way that is accessible to everybody (no mean feat given what they’re adapting). The baker is absolutely hilarious, as is the hypochondriac butcher who is, with probably the most occupationally unfortunate phobia ever, afraid of blood. The portrayal of the bonnet maker, by an obviously very talented young actress, includes a French accent that would convince anybody (apart from the Billiard player) of its authenticity. The play serves as a good introduction to The Snark for anybody not too familiar to the text, but those who are familiar will also find plenty to enjoy.
