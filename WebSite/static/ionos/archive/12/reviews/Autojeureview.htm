Tramps Like Us

Autojeu Theatre


Autojeu Theatre returns to Theatrefest with another fine performance - the same cast and a brand new work in progress. Faced with confrontation between two ‘down and outs’, we are left to ponder on whether it is possible or even acceptable to have hierarchy and racism amongst the tramp fraternity. We are regaled with rather tall stories of who is most deserving of this ‘space’; the Muslim genie (watch for Rowan Atkinson/Mr Bean facial expressions!) or our very own gallant St George. A bitter sweet comedy which leaves us with the feeling of ‘there for the grace of God go I’.  This comedy duo compliment each other with perfect precision.
Fringe Secrets

 

Autojeu's second outing with a two man performance is a follow up to the highly acclaimed and very popular 'How To Climb Mount Everest' which appeared at last year's TheatreFest.

'Tramps Like Us' takes a light-hearted look at the politics of the homeless and how they conquer their patch. Although in its infancy, this piece shows real promise of becoming another popular show and rightly so. Sam Gibbs & Nusret Ozguc have a great chemistry between them which is a joy to observe and they do what they do well. With stories about Deer, St George and Muslim Genies this is a show worth catching before the guys shoot off to Edinburgh. If you need any other reason to catch this show then do note that when a show about Tramps gains a Thumbs Up verdict from a former homeless gentleman who was in the audience they must be doing something right!
Liam Gifford
