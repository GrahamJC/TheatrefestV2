The Chocolate Paradox

Plain Quakers


In this hard-hitting, funny yet poignant look at the worst of un-regulated capitalist excess and globalisation, a group of ordinary Yorkshire friends get caught up in different ways with modern economy and its pitfalls. When Albert, reveals an interest in Quakers, his friend Morris decides to take part in a local play detailing the life of George Cadbury; probably the world’s most famous Quaker. Is Quakerism the antidote to unethical and over complicated financial practice? Can Quaker principles be reconciled with the harsh dog-eat-dog world of cooperate power? How can chocolate contain chemicals that cause feelings of love and elation, whilst its makers are in poverty? This play will challenge you to look at your buying habits and maybe even inspire you to make a change.
