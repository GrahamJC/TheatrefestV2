Graveside Manner

Bill Jones


I’ll be honest. Graveside Manner is not going to be everybody’s cup of tea. And why should it be?

The whole performance centres around an incredibly miserable man lamenting his woeful life and reading dark poetry. And do you know what? I thought it was hilarious! There was a real sense of schadenfreude from the audience who were creased up in laughter as we listened to Miserable Malcolm regale us with the breakdown of his relationship with Mavis and her new gym instructor boyfriend, Dave. Who’d have thought so many words rhymed with Dave?

Each morbid and dark word was spoken with such gloom and doom that it really was hard to tell if we were watching a performance or attending a counselling session for this poor man. I think he wanted us to feel sorry for him….I didn’t. Or perhaps that was his intention as no matter how depressing Malcom’s poems became, the audience were laughing more and more. A highlight for me was the “Melancholics Anonymous” section which left me sore with laughter.

Graveside Manner is certainly like Marmite. You’ll either love it or hate it. And that’s exactly what great and inspiring theatre should do…..divide opinion.

This reviewer LOVED it!
Billy Buckingham, The Daily Fringe
