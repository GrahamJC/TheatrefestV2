Death and the Data Processor

The Cock and Bulls


Murder, madness and mayhem all the way! Be prepared to get up close and cosy with a loud, rude, overbearing yet well intentioned sock puppet named Vincent from the word 'Go'! But Vincent is just one of a fast and furious collection of characters who skilfully weave their way in and out of this ingenious performance at breakneck speed. A completely bonkers story – great fun for older teens and adults alike with plenty of giggling going on in the audience. Colourful language and innuendo might raise an eyebrow at times... but hey, it's all part of the feel of this delightfully surreal performance.

Poor Ian finds himself a murder suspect transported to Harton, a curiously happy commune for fugitive criminals of all types (“No crime too great or small”) where he meets a variety of crazy and somewhat volatile characters cleverly portrayed by the two central actors with masks, cardboard boxes, socks and fluffy toys and finds himself involved in one hilarious disembowelling after another.

A great team, who engaged comfortably with their audience in the tiny space that is St Anne's on this balmy first night.

You very possibly won't have seen anything quite like it before – and you certainly can't afford to take your eye off the ball for even a moment as the actors charge their way through this fast paced, chaotic performance!

'INVINCENTABLE' quite frankly!
Lesley Gaish, The Daily Fringe
