The Sentence

Lytton Productions


Sombre. Compelling. Moving. St Anne's provided a truly atmospheric backdrop for this cleverly structured drama exploring language and justice – and the fate of those who have dared to challenge established authority in our country.

The audience was cleverly transported to the time and place in 1538 when John Scrivener was sentenced to be burnt at the stake – and his daughter, Joan, compelled to light the fire – for his crime of daring to translate the Bible into English.

Powerful and thought provoking, the evocative setting and Lytton's use of quotes from the Bible and strong narrative illuminated and strengthened this impressive historical performance.

The penetrating rhetoric gave real insight into the perilous journey of our ancestors towards our eventual freedom to stand before this Book and read its powerful message without fear of reprisal.

The audience loved it. Resounding applause for this superb performance reached into every last corner of this ancient building - well written, intelligent and beautifully performed.

Bravo!
Lesley Gaish, The Daily Fringe
