The jewel you lost was blue

Sapta Dance Company


When nine over 60’s came together and responded to a single line of the poem “Red” by Ted Hughes, a beautiful interpretation was formed.

Sapta Dance’s beautifully choreographed piece was carefully constructed through a combination of lighting, dance and sound. This cocktail of techniques allowed the creation of an emotive sequence of events, which fully engaged the audience in their story.

The absence of speech was barely noticed due to the variation in intensity, pace and genre of the music which allowed for the emotions of those on stage to be effectively communicated to the audience. Alongside this, the fluidity and complexity of their movements clearly demonstrated their passion and drive to perform, meaning that the audience connected to both the actors on stage and to the story that they were portraying.

The audience gained perspective when watching this performance due to the clever interaction between the actors, costume and props. Copious amounts of symbolism were present on stage, particularly within the use of every-day items, such as books, kettles, shoes and lipstick.

I recommend watching this performance as it stunningly captures the importance of individuality and staying true to yourself, something which everyone should be reminded of.
Tasmin Sargeant, The Daily Fringe
