Barum's Secrets and Lies

Entertainingly Different Youth Theatre


Barum's Secrets and Lies began slow and the actors seemed nervous, but before long the young cast took hold of the stage, making it their own and creating a great atmosphere. Devon’s own Entertainingly Different Youth Theatre takes the audience on a fantastic heart-warming story of age and times past, bringing the secrets of Barnstaple’s history to life. This being said it would have been nice to see a little more of the history. Adapted from a play by Penny Griffiths, Barum's Secrets and Lies boasts both a funny script and clever characters. John, a young trouble maker is forced to do community service at an old people’s home where he meets the old Mr. Peters, a war veteran and huge fan of Barnstaple’s own John Gay, author of The Beggar's Opera and an unlikely friendship emerges. The set is simple, yet effective with music that suits the story well. Over all this was a funny, well produced well-acted play./p>
