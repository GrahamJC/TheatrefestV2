A Corner of the Ocean

Jammy Voo

Physical theatre, puppetry and live music unravel the mystery of a diver’s disappearance that ripples through the lives of four women… a darkly, comic look at our everyday struggles to keep a head above water.

 

The buzz from Edinburgh Fringe 2010:
Vibrant physical theatre that's set to make waves...a production that has both depth and sparkle and reminds you just how refreshing original theatre can be. (THE LIST)

Perhaps the most technically accomplished piece of puppetry I saw at this year’s Fringe (ANIMATIONS ONLINE)

The company's greatest strength is its ability to tell stories visually...invigorating moments of physicality, live music and puppetry (THE SCOTSMAN)

I loved ‘A Corner of the Ocean’ for daring to be something a bit different at this year’s Fringe. It was like a breath of fresh air (AUDIENCE MEMBER)
