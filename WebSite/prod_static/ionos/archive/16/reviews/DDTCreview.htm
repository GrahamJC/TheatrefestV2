Shakespeare Avengers Assembleth: Age of Oberon

Drake's Drummers Theatre Company


What happens when you take four (easy on the eye) young men and allow them to explore the possibilities that come with Shakespeare's writing?

Fifty-five minutes of laughter, fun and captivating story arcs interwoven with quick costume changes, this unlikely and strange force of characters set out on a mission to save Shakespeare when they're left with nothing but a note and a feather. However all is not as it seems, as they come to realize. The feather is actually the infinity quill - and it's already fallen into the wrong hands (despite Shakespeare's clear instructions). The relationships are put to the test when the avengers are forced to split up and journey through the enchanted forest, in search for their creator.

Overall this performance was a fun and enjoyable interpretation that I would happily go and watch again. I would recommend this performance for all ages as it provides a humorous display of Shakespeare's characters, a vague knowledge of Shakespeare's writing comes in handy but overall the jokes can be universally understood.

I am impressed with the clear differences in setting that were achieved despite the same minimalistic set, and the strong personalities that were communicated for all eight characters within the short amount of time.
Tasmin Sargeant, The Daily Fringe
