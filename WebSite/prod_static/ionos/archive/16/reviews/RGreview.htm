Forget Me Not – The Alzheimer’s Whodunnit

Rob Gee


Forget Me Not – The Alzheimer’s Whodunnit is an intriguing murder mystery set in the very unconventional location of an Alzheimer’s ward.

The story’s combination of serious themes, its exploration of the personal tragedies of the patients and the death that sets the murder mystery in motion might have created a very sombre performance had it not been for Forget Me Not’s use of comedic poems and characters.

The poetry that helped shape the mystery was both well written and very funny, and judging from the number of people who purchased Rob Gee’s poems after the performance was over, I wasn’t the only one that felt that way.

Every aspect of this show fits together perfectly: the music, characters, comedic poems, compelling story and even Rob Gee’s delivery all act as a good counterbalance to the thought-provoking and emotive bedrock of Forget Me Not-The Alzheimer’s Who Dunnit.

All in all I cannot think of a single thing that Rob gee could have done better in forget me not: the story was great, the poems were hilarious, the characters were interesting, the setting was unique and the music was well chosen, so this production gets a strong recommendation because it does everything right and exceeds already high expectations.
George Chapman, The Daily Fringe
