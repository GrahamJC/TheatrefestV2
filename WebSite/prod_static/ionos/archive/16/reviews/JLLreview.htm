Just Charming

James Luckraft-Law


“Once upon a time there was a man who lived at home with his parents and worked in a shop.”

We are all aware of the great heroic acts that come along-side the title of being a prince. More often than not it requires saving an entire kingdom – including damsels in distress. But what if this is no longer a requirement?

This performances’ focus is not so much on the inherent greatness that comes with being royalty, but the trials and tribulations that come with being human.

This witty one-man show delves into the relatable struggles faced when you are an average human being in search of your fairy tale ending - when crowns and swords are not so much your forte as vodka and terrible dates. Lacking in both selfconfidence and the financial ability to own his own horse, we follow this individuals’ journey of thoughts as he questions his role within his personal kingdom.

It’s safe to say that we may not be about to hang out the bunting for this modern day prince (in many aspects of his life) but his comical reflection on his experiences, as well as predictions for his future, has us all rooting for his happy ever after.
Tasmin Sargeant, The Daily Fringe
