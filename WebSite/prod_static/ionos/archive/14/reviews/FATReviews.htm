Seven Missed Meals Leads to Anarchy

Four Average Tomatoes Theatre Company


‘Seven Missed Meals Leads to Anarchy’ is a quirky but truthful title. It is portrayed by a quartet with contrasting roles: two are unwitting gameshow contestants, the other two are the hosts. Mr C., (Mr Conscience) the male presenter bursts onto the scene like sunshine complete with orange tan and a matching shirt that screams Hawaiian beach. However, the tone of the show is less than cheerful. This show is a deliberately politically motivated challenge to the huge unscrupulous food industry on which we all depend.

The cheerful daytime TV style of the spoof gameshow is appropriately ironic and as a result the humorous content makes the audience feel awkward. The factual content is certainly 'food' for thought and gave 'substance' to the performance, and provides a few food waste issues to ponder over. In addition, the use of the contestants as human puppets by Mr C.’s glamourous assistant was executed cleanly and effectively. ‘Seven Missed Meals Leads to Anarchy’ didn’t entertain, but is informing and could develop into a truely challenging performance.
Jackson Briggs, The Daily Fringe
