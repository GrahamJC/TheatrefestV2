Hammering Near Glass

CAKE Productions


Hammering Near Glass is a psychological thriller created by CAKE productions. It presents the themes of empathy, confidentiality and unconditional love with a sinister twist. The plot revolves around an apparent overdose that entangles the lives of three very different individuals: a sympathetic social worker who sees the good in everyone, a sceptical policeman with a passion for solving puzzles and a very troubled suspect with a history of self harm. Because of its subject matter, this is not the kind of production that viewers should go to if they are looking for something uplifting or light-hearted. What the audience gets instead is a moving, powerful and thought provoking performance.

All of the actors in Hammering Near Glass captivate their audience from the first scene and the performance keeps their attention for the entire seventy minutes that make up this production. To summarise, Hammering Near Glass gives its audience a poignant glimpse of the crushing burden that confidentiality can be, of how unconditional love can quickly change into something much more unpleasant and of the sometimes naive nature of empathy.
George Chapman, The Daily Fringe
