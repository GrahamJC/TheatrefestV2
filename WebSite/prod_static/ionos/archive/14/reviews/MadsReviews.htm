Armstrong: Revolutionists(s)

Mads Floor Andersen & Sam Gibbs


“Do you ever take the time to look at the stars?” A simple question leads us into the peaceful bubble Mads Floor-Andersen and Sam Gibbs have expertly created. Using beautifully eloquent prose, the duo create a series of gorgeous images, both imaginary and real, to place their musical footprints. We are eased into the piece tastefully, courtesy of Flying Saucers and Space Invaders, being gently guided on an expedition to find where space and music meet and our place within the vastness. Anyone who has seen any of Sam Gibbs’ work before will know the ease with which he is able to make audiences laugh and relax, and once again he does not disappoint. His talent is matched by Mads’ visual creative skill, turning simple acts into things of wonder. This charming pairing produces a piece that is able to easily move from playful popping candy rockets, to touching moments of recollection with great warmth and sophistication. Sweet, funny and tender, if you want to embark on a journey into inner space, these two are the perfect companions. A brilliant performance by two very gifted artists.
Sapphire Urwick, The Daily Fringe
