venues

The Baptist Hall is at the top end of Boutport Street, next to the Baptist Church. This fine, historic building provides a large enough space to accommodate a generous 60 seat studio theatre, big enough for dance and some of the larger-cast extravaganzas.

 

Room Upstairs is a newly refurbished room on the First Floor of the Baptist Hall providing an intimate space for a select 20 at a time. This is a Pay-What-You-Will venue. Places can be reserved 30 minutes before a performance.

 

The Guildhall comes thanks to Barnstaple Town Council. The programme of shows is sponsored by Samuels Solicitors. The Guildhall holds 80 and is a Pay-What-You-Will venue. Places can be reserved 30 minutes before a show.

 

St Anne's Arts & Community Centre provides an informal 50 seat setting for a number of particularly innovative pieces of work. The building itself is well worth a visit, steeped, as it is, in history. Barnstaple Town Council have made St Anne's available.

 

The Golden Lion Tap is an old coaching inn that borders The Square. The management have generously made available the large back room that is well-known in the town as a music venue. This will be configured as a studio/cabaret space with room for 60.

 

The Tent on the Square will be a 60-seat venue housing shows suitable for all the family. It will also be a space for workshops and a wet-weather provision for street shows. The box-office for the Tent will be situated just across the street in The Golden Lion Tap.

 

Thereare a substantial number of other less formal venues both outside and inside which you will find on the map.
