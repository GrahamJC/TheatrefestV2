looking forward to 2019




SOME IMPORTANT DATES

Fringe TheatreFest '19 will run from Thursday to Sunday, 27 - 30 June.

Applications to take part in a regular venue will be accepted from Monday 19th November until Friday 14th December. The draw will take place on Saturday 15th December.

Applications to take part in an alternative space (Found Venues and Outside Locations) will be accepted between Monday 19th November and Friday March 22nd. Productions will be accepted on a first-come-first-served basis and there is a limit on the number of productions that we can accept, so best to get applications in early.

See the performers page for application details.


what is Fringe TheatreFest?

Fringe TheatreFest is a low-cost opportunity to savour a whole variety of theatre. There are professional companies at the top of their game, community companies presenting ambitious work, aspiring professionals, schools, colleges, youth theatres and other groupings each with their own reasons for taking part. All share a passion to communicate. The festival is not juried, curated or censored. So the programme doesn't conform to a single vision of how theatre should be made or what it should contain but instead presents a wide range of approaches.

Fringe TheatreFest '19 will feature seven ticketed venues plus a raft of shows in alternative spaces that charge on a pay-what-you-will basis.

There will be around 250 performances of over 70 different shows in just 4 days.

There’ll be comedy, tragedy, dance, spoken word, music, circus and puppetry in a multitude of combinations - something for everybody!


Fringe TheatreFest is supported by a large group of experienced volunteers - many professionals amongst them - who provide box-office, stewarding and technical expertise.

Fringe TheatreFest attracts invaluable backing from sponsors and grant-giving bodies as well as businesses, schools and other institutions that help us in kind.
